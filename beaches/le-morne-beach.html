Le Morne Beach

Mauritius Southwest Coast Indian Ocean White Sand

Le Morne Beach stretches along the southwestern peninsula of Mauritius, at the foot of Le Morne Brabant, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The mountain holds deep historical significance as a refuge for escaped enslaved people in the 18th and 19th centuries. The beach itself is renowned for its clear lagoon, consistent trade winds ideal for kite-surfing, and views of the "underwater waterfall" optical illusion offshore.

Access

Fly into Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (MRU). Le Morne is approximately 45 km southwest, reachable by rental car or taxi in under an hour. Public buses also service the area.

Historical Significance

Le Morne Brabant served as a shelter for runaway enslaved people (maroons) during the French and early British colonial periods. The mountain was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2008 in recognition of this history. Visitors should approach the site with awareness of its cultural weight.
