Madagascar

Indian Ocean East Africa 4,828 km Coastline

Madagascar, the world's fourth-largest island, lies in the Indian Ocean off Africa's southeast coast. Its 4,800+ km coastline includes tropical islands in the northwest (Nosy Be), the whale-watching paradise of ÃŽle Sainte-Marie in the east, and remote southern beaches near Fort Dauphin. The island's famous biodiversity—90% endemic species—extends to its marine environments, though coastal infrastructure remains limited.

Coastline Character

The northwest coast (Nosy Be archipelago) offers calm, warm waters ideal for beach tourism. The east coast faces the open Indian Ocean with more dramatic seas but spectacular humpback whale migrations (July–September). The southwest has wilder, less-visited beaches backed by spiny forest. Coral reefs fringe much of the coastline.
